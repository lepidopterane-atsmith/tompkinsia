The Verse End Formula

The verbatim claim: “ἔπος ηὔδα: a VE formula (14x Il., 1x Od.); always with a preceding participle that determines the tone of the following speech more precisely (ὁμοκλήσας only here; elsewhere δακρύσας, εὐχόμενος, ὀλοφὐρομενος, etc.).”

Our interpretation: 1) If ἔπος ηὔδα is a VE formula, because VE means verse end, these words are found next to each other at the end of a line, and 2) ἔπος ηὔδα will always be preceded within the line by a participle.

Using the treebanks, we looked up each result and evaluated 1) whether these words were at the end of a line, and 2) whether the phrase was preceded by a participle. (Have you ever noticed how you can visit a website like this one or this one and replace 0012-001 with any other Document URN? This is one tip that can help you look these over for yourself.) Anyway, while walking through 12 examples would be tedious, it is important to note that while we did find all our instances at the end of a line, the first two instances on this list had no accompanying participle, so it might be better to say that ἔπος ηὔδα is usually rather than always with a preceding participle.
